hello and welcome to the fourth day of the second week
today we will do something a little bit different
after staying with the breath for a little while we will try to directly observe our thoughts as they come and go
if you are ready you can put your feet flat on the floor
hands on your knees
keeping your back straight without straining too much finding its natural relaxed straight position
check if you are sitting comfortably
does anything need to change before we go into our practice
all right and then you can give one final signal to your body that for now you can really relax
okay now that we are here focus on your belly taking some deep breaths
now notice the spot at the nose where the air is really noticeably passing
stay there for a while to help you stay there you can count the breaths the same way we have been doing before gently noting distractions appreciating them for what they are and letting them go
now letting go of counting we say in with the in breath out with the out breath
gently noting distractions we can now stay with the sensation of the breath in the nose without any counting or words
now instead of taking our breath as the point to return to
we will gently observe our stream of thought watching thoughts as they come and go
not trying to change them just sitting back seeing how long our attention can stay in this observing position naturally
without getting carried away with any particular train of thought
what many people find a helpful analogy here is to imagine themselves sitting outside on a cloudy day
watching clouds gently drift by with each thought you are having represented by one such cloud
some thoughts really want to draw you in and want you to think about them further losing sight of the whole sky
that is okay that is part of how our mind is
that can be very useful
for now just see if you can without too much effort stay there watching observing the thoughts from the outside as they want to draw you in
can you notice your immediate reactions to observing them
can you allow whatever reaction to be
can you be kind with those reactions in a way that allows you to come back to watching those thoughts come and go
the same as with listening to the sounds no need to run around trying to hunt or capture thoughts
just sit back stay there waiting patiently for anything to come on its own if it wants to
gently observing it if we let our minds be carried away
acknowledging the distraction for what it is letting go of it
slowly coming back to your calmly observing position
for some people it can also be helpful to sound out the thought to verbalize it without judging it after saying it in your mind you don't try to analyze it just let it go and continue to observe
sitting here you can also take a moment to notice what is it like to sit there watching your thoughts as they come and go
take a moment to let a word for this experience come on its own some word that resonates
okay then you can slowly come back to the space that you are in the body that you are in
take a moment to appreciate your effort today in trying out this exercise
i hope you enjoyed taking our thoughts as the main point of focus today and i will see you again tomorrow
